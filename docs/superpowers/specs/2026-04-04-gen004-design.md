# Gen 004 Design: Gate Loosening, Universe Expansion, 30-Day Cycle Evaluation

**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Status:** Draft
**Generation:** gen_004
**Predecessor:** gen_003 (10-strategy ag enhancement)

---

## Motivation

Gen 003 added USDA, Drought Monitor, and expanded ag tickers. Three systemic issues remain:

1. **Restrictive gates** — Hard thresholds in `screen()` methods reject ambiguous-but-tradeable signals before the LLM ever sees them. The LLM is best at weighing ambiguous signals in combination, but it never gets the chance.
2. **Narrow ticker universes** — Hardcoded lists on weather_ag (10 instruments), govt_contracts (14 contractors), and state_economics (5 ETFs) miss adjacent opportunities.
3. **No portfolio evaluation cadence** — No concept of evaluation cycles. Performance is measured ad hoc. Strategies have inconsistent holding periods (7-30 days) that don't align with a coherent portfolio investment horizon.

## Design Overview

Four independent workstreams that compose cleanly:

1. **Gate Loosening** — Lower thresholds on all 10 strategies. Gates answer "is there data?" not "is this tradeable?"
2. **Universe Expansion** — Hybrid curated core + dynamic OpenBB sector expansion for hardcoded-universe strategies.
3. **OpenBB Availability Enforcement** — Loud degradation warnings, infrastructure health reporting.
4. **30-Day Cycle Evaluation + Horizon Alignment** — New `CycleTracker` for observation, plus calibrate all strategies to a 30-day investment horizon.

---

## Workstream 1: Gate Loosening

### Principle

Gates should be coarse "is there enough data to form an opinion?" checks. The LLM — via portfolio committee enrichment — is the conviction filter. Each strategy's `screen()` still returns at most `max_positions` candidates, so LLM cost does not increase.

### Strategy-by-Strategy Changes

#### WeatherAg (`strategies/weather_ag.py`)

**Current:** drought_composite >= 1.0 OR heat_stress_days >= 5 OR precip_deficit < -25%

**Proposed:** drought_composite >= 0.3 OR heat_stress_days >= 2 OR precip_deficit < -10% OR crop_decline >= 1pp OR any ag ticker momentum > 2%

Rationale: A drought score of 0.8 with moderate heat and slight precip deficit could be meaningful in combination. The current thresholds reject these compound signals.

#### EarningsCall (`strategies/earnings_call.py`)

**Current:** Hard EPS surprise threshold.

**Proposed:** Any non-zero EPS surprise (positive or negative). Analyst consensus boost remains but is not a gate.

Rationale: Small surprises in context (e.g., a 2% miss during sector rotation) can be significant. Let the LLM judge context.

#### GovtContracts (`strategies/govt_contracts.py`)

**Current:** Contract amount > $50M AND momentum > 2%.

**Proposed:** Contract amount > $10M. Drop momentum gate entirely.

Rationale: Momentum is irrelevant to the thesis (government contract = future revenue). Smaller contracts ($10-50M) can be material for mid-cap defense names.

#### InsiderActivity (`strategies/insider_activity.py`)

**Current:** Cluster size >= 3 buys.

**Proposed:** Cluster size >= 2 buys.

Rationale: Two C-suite officers buying simultaneously is highly informative. Requiring 3 filters out most insider clusters.

#### Litigation (`strategies/litigation.py`)

**Current:** Case must match SIGNAL_NATURES set (specific nature_of_suit codes).

**Proposed:** Any case where a party name matches a known public company (matched against the OpenBB company name list or a local ticker-to-name mapping). SIGNAL_NATURES becomes a score boost rather than a gate.

Rationale: Novel case types (e.g., AI copyright disputes) don't match existing nature codes but may be market-moving. Company matching uses the same fuzzy matching logic as GovtContracts ticker resolution.

#### RegulatoryPipeline (`strategies/regulatory_pipeline.py`)

**Current:** Agency whitelist: SEC, EPA, FDA, FTC.

**Proposed:** All agencies. Filter by comment period recency (proposed rules with comment period closing within 60 days).

Rationale: DOE, DOT, CFPB, OSHA rules can all move sectors. The whitelist was arbitrary.

#### SupplyChain (`strategies/supply_chain.py`)

**Current:** Headline + summary must contain DISRUPTION_KEYWORDS.

**Proposed:** Any Finnhub news with category "supply chain" OR DISRUPTION_KEYWORDS present. Expand DISRUPTION_KEYWORDS to include "tariff", "sanction", "embargo", "port congestion", "inventory", "backlog".

Rationale: Keyword gate misses novel disruption language. Category-based filtering catches broader signals.

#### FilingAnalysis (`strategies/filing_analysis.py`)

**Current:** Form types: 10-K, 10-Q, DEF 14A.

**Proposed:** Add 8-K (material events), S-1 (IPOs/secondaries), SC 13D (activist stakes), SC 13G (passive large stakes).

Rationale: 8-K filings are the most time-sensitive. SC 13D signals activist involvement, a well-documented alpha source.

#### CongressionalTrades (`strategies/congressional_trades.py`)

**Current:** min_amount_bucket >= 2.

**Proposed:** min_amount_bucket >= 1 (include smallest disclosed trades).

Rationale: Members sometimes split large positions across disclosure buckets. The smallest bucket still represents meaningful conviction for a public official.

#### StateEconomics (`strategies/state_economics.py`)

**Current:** Combined score >= min_return threshold.

**Proposed:** Pass all ETFs to LLM with momentum + FRED context. Let LLM rank rather than applying a hard minimum return cutoff.

Rationale: A negative-momentum ETF during a regime shift could be a contrarian signal. The hard floor prevents this.

## Workstream 2: Universe Expansion

### Principle

Hybrid approach: curated core tickers (always present) + dynamic expansion via OpenBB industry classification (when available). Dynamic event-driven strategies (earnings, insider, litigation, supply chain, regulatory, congressional) are unchanged — they already scan whatever comes through their data feeds with no artificial limitations.

### WeatherAg Universe

**Current core (10):** DBA, CORN, WEAT, SOYB, MOO, ADM, BG, DE, AGCO, CF

**Add curated (8):** PEP, KO, GIS, MDLZ (food/beverage), MOS, NTR, FMC (fertilizer/crop chem), CTVA (crop science)

**Dynamic expansion industries:** "Agricultural Products", "Packaged Foods", "Farm & Heavy Construction Machinery", "Agricultural Inputs"

**Resulting universe:** 18 curated + dynamic sector tickers (deduplicated). Seasonal filtering still applies (full universe Apr-Sep, winter subset Oct-Mar — winter subset expanded proportionally).

### GovtContracts Universe

**Current core (14):** Existing CONTRACTOR_TICKERS dict

**Add curated (9):** PLTR, SNOW, MDB (defense tech/cloud), BWX, HEI, TDG (defense components), CACI, SAIC, LDOS (IT services)

**Dynamic expansion industries:** "Aerospace & Defense", "Information Technology Services"

**Ticker resolution:** Keep recipient name → ticker mapping. Add fuzzy matching for recipient names not in the curated list (Levenshtein distance or substring match against OpenBB company names).

### StateEconomics Universe

**Current core (5):** KRE, IWN, XRT, IYR, XHB

**Add curated (5):** ITB (homebuilders), VNQ (broader REIT), SOXX (semiconductors/regional manufacturing), XLI (industrials), XLRE (real estate select sector)

**No dynamic expansion** — ETFs are a finite, curated set. 10 total is comprehensive enough for regional/sector macro signals.

### Implementation Pattern

Each hardcoded-universe strategy gets a `get_universe()` method:

```python
def get_universe(self, openbb_source=None) -> list[str]:
    universe = set(self.CORE_TICKERS)
    if openbb_source and openbb_source.is_available():
        for industry in self.EXPANSION_INDUSTRIES:
            tickers = openbb_source.fetch({
                "method": "sector_tickers",
                "industry": industry
            })
            universe.update(tickers)
    blocked = set(self.config.get("blocked_tickers", []))
    return sorted(universe - blocked)
```

Graceful degradation: if OpenBB is unavailable, fall back to curated core list only. This is a degraded state (see Workstream 3).

### OpenBB `sector_tickers` Method

New method on `OpenBBSource`:

- **Input:** `{"method": "sector_tickers", "industry": "Agricultural Products"}`
- **Output:** List of ticker strings for all companies in that industry
- **Source:** OpenBB equity screener by industry classification
- **Cache:** 24-hour TTL (industry membership doesn't change intraday)
- **Fallback:** Empty list if OpenBB unavailable

## Workstream 3: OpenBB Availability Enforcement

### Problem

OpenBB is treated as optional everywhere — silent degradation via `if openbb_source and openbb_source.is_available()`. With dynamic universe expansion depending on it, silent degradation means the system runs in a crippled state without anyone noticing.

### Changes

#### Startup Validation

At `CohortOrchestrator` initialization, check `openbb_source.is_available()`. If unavailable:
- Log `WARNING: OpenBB unavailable — running in degraded mode (curated universes only, no enrichment)`
- Set `self.openbb_degraded = True`
- Continue execution (don't crash)

#### Infrastructure Health in Daily Reports

Add an "Infrastructure Health" section to `generate_daily_report.py` output:

```
## Infrastructure Health
| Source        | Status | Notes                    |
|---------------|--------|--------------------------|
| OpenBB        | OK     |                          |
| NOAA CDO      | OK     |                          |
| USDA NASS     | DEGRADED | API key missing        |
| Drought Monitor | OK   |                          |
| FRED          | OK     |                          |
```

Each data source's `is_available()` method is already implemented — this just surfaces the results.

#### Signal Journal Metadata

Each signal logged to the journal includes `openbb_available: bool`. This allows post-hoc analysis: correlate hit rates with/without OpenBB enrichment to measure its value.

#### No Hard Failure

OpenBB unavailability never crashes the system. Curated core lists always work. But degradation is loud — visible in logs, reports, and journal metadata.

## Workstream 4: 30-Day Cycle Evaluation + Horizon Alignment

### 4A: Strategy Horizon Alignment

#### Principle

Every signal should answer "why will this move price within 30 days?" Each strategy's default holding period, LLM prompt, and exit logic should be calibrated for this horizon.

#### Holding Period Calibration

| Strategy | Current Default | Proposed Default | Rationale |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| EarningsCall | 7 days | 15-20 days | Post-earnings drift is documented over 2-3 weeks. 7 days exits before the drift completes. |
| SupplyChain | 10 days | 20-25 days | Supply disruptions take weeks to price across the chain. 10 days only captures initial reaction. |
| FilingAnalysis | 15 days | 25-30 days | Filing implications (10-K risk factors, activist 13D) unfold over weeks as analysts digest. |
| Litigation | 15 days | 25-30 days | Court filing impact + analyst reaction takes ~1 month. Cases don't resolve, but sentiment shifts do. |
| InsiderActivity | 20 days | 25-30 days | Insider signal decay is ~30 days in academic literature. |
| WeatherAg | 21 days | 25-30 days | Crop/weather impacts take 3-4 weeks to flow into commodity prices and earnings expectations. |
| CongressionalTrades | 25 days | 25-30 days | Minor adjustment, already close to aligned. |
| GovtContracts | 30 days | 30 days | Already aligned. No change. |
| RegulatoryPipeline | 30 days | 30 days | Already aligned. No change. |
| StateEconomics | 14-day rebalance | 30-day rebalance | Align rebalance window with evaluation cycle. |

#### Parameter Range Adjustment

All strategies: `max_holding_days` parameter range shifts to floor 20 days, ceiling 45 days. This prevents the learning loop from evolving holding periods that are too short for the 30-day thesis while allowing some overshoot without forcing premature exits.

#### LLM Prompt Changes

Each strategy's `build_propose_prompt()` and the portfolio committee system prompt explicitly frame the horizon:

> "Given a 30-day investment horizon, how confident are you that this signal will generate meaningful price movement within that window? Consider the typical timeline for this type of catalyst to be reflected in price."

This replaces any existing horizon-agnostic framing.

#### What This Does NOT Mean

- Positions are not forced closed at day 30
- Early exits on stop-loss or take-profit remain unchanged
- Strategy-specific exit logic (e.g., GovtContracts profit target) remains unchanged
- The `max_holding_days` ceiling of 45 days allows natural thesis completion beyond 30 days

### 4B: CycleTracker

#### Purpose

Observation-only component that produces portfolio evaluation snapshots at 30-day boundaries aligned to generation start date.

#### Cycle Definition

- Cycle 1: `gen_start_date` to `gen_start_date + 29 days` (inclusive)
- Cycle 2: `gen_start_date + 30 days` to `gen_start_date + 59 days`
- Cycle N: `gen_start_date + (N-1)*30` to `gen_start_date + N*30 - 1`
- Current cycle: `((current_date - gen_start_date).days) // 30 + 1`
- Days remaining: `30 - ((current_date - gen_start_date).days % 30)`

#### Location

- **New file:** `tradingagents/autoresearch/cycle_tracker.py`
- **State:** `data/generations/gen_NNN/{control,adaptive}/cycles/cycle_NNN.json`

#### Class Interface

```python
class CycleTracker:
    def __init__(self, gen_start_date: str, state_dir: str):
        """Initialize with generation start date and state directory."""

    def current_cycle(self, trading_date: str) -> int:
        """Return the current cycle number (1-indexed)."""

    def days_remaining(self, trading_date: str) -> int:
        """Days remaining in the current cycle."""

    def is_boundary(self, trading_date: str) -> bool:
        """True if trading_date is the last day of a cycle."""

    def update_daily(self, trading_date: str, positions: list, portfolio_value: float):
        """Called after each trading day. Updates running metrics."""

    def snapshot_cycle(self, cycle_number: int, positions: list,
                       closed_trades: list, portfolio_value: float) -> dict:
        """Generate full cycle evaluation. Called on boundary days."""
```

#### Cycle Snapshot Contents

```json
{
    "cycle": 2,
    "start_date": "2026-05-01",
    "end_date": "2026-05-30",
    "portfolio_value_start": 5000.00,
    "portfolio_value_end": 5234.50,
    "cycle_return_pct": 4.69,
    "realized_pnl": 180.00,
    "unrealized_pnl": 54.50,
    "positions_opened": 12,
    "positions_closed": 9,
    "positions_open_at_end": 4,
    "capital_utilization_avg_pct": 62.3,
    "capital_utilization_peak_pct": 85.1,
    "strategy_breakdown": {
        "earnings_call": {"signals": 3, "traded": 2, "pnl": 85.00, "hit_rate": 0.67, "avg_hold_days": 18},
        "weather_ag": {"signals": 5, "traded": 1, "pnl": -12.50, "hit_rate": 0.0, "avg_hold_days": 25}
    },
    "cohort": "adaptive"
}
```

#### Integration Points

- **Engine:** Called at the end of `run_paper_trade_phase()`, after exits and before learning.
- **Daily report:** `generate_daily_report.py` includes "Cycle N, Day X of 30" in the header. On boundary days, the cycle evaluation summary is appended.
- **Generation comparison:** `generation_comparison.py` can compare cycles across generations (e.g., gen_003 cycle 1 vs gen_004 cycle 1).

#### Non-goals (Potential Follow-on)

- Cycle context injection into portfolio committee prompts (e.g., "15 days remaining, prefer shorter-hold signals")
- Automatic strategy parameter adjustment between cycles
- Forced liquidation at cycle boundaries

## Cost Impact

- **LLM cost:** Minimal increase. Gate loosening sends more candidates to the LLM, but `max_positions` caps still apply. Wider universes mean more tickers to screen, but screening is rule-based (no LLM).
- **API cost:** `sector_tickers` calls are cached 24h. One call per industry per day, ~4 industries = 4 additional OpenBB calls/day.
- **Memory:** Universe expansion adds at most ~50 tickers per strategy. Negligible on 16GB M4.

## Risks

- **Looser gates = more noise:** Mitigated by `max_positions` caps and LLM scoring. Monitor hit rates in the first cycle.
- **Dynamic universes include illiquid names:** Mitigated by the existing risk gate minimum position size ($100) and the portfolio committee's confidence scoring. Very small companies will get low confidence.
- **OpenBB `sector_tickers` may not map cleanly to our industries:** Validate the industry classification strings against OpenBB's actual taxonomy during implementation.
